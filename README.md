An R&D project on a compact, modular **heat-pump-powered dehydrator** designed for off-grid agricultural drying of spices, fruits, fish, and grains. Combines **first-principles psychrometric sizing** with **SolidWorks CAD** and **economic payback modelling** to deliver a window-unit form factor system with linear capacity scaling through modular parallel-unit architecture.
 
## Overview
 
The project addresses post-harvest crop loss for smallholder farmers in tropical regions, where existing drying options all carry serious limitations:
 
- Sun drying — free, but weather-dependent, slow, and exposes produce to contamination
- Resistive electric dryers — consume ~2–3 kWh per kg of moisture removed
- LPG / biomass dryers — fuel cost volatility and direct emissions
- None scale linearly: more capacity means buying a whole new system
 
The system targets three properties:
 
- **Energy-efficient** — heat pump cycle moves heat rather than generating it
- **Standalone** — window-unit form factor for non-specialist installation
- **Modular** — parallel-unit architecture for capacity scaling
 
## Highlights
 
- Compact window-unit form factor with adjustable air outlet and integrated control panel
- **~64% energy reduction** vs equivalent resistive heating, validated by thermal model
- Dual heating + dehumidification from a single refrigeration cycle
- Modular parallel-unit architecture for linear capacity scaling
- SolidWorks CAD, thermal sizing, and economic payback analysis complete
- AI-generated concept render developed for visual communication
- Design patent application in preparation (India)

## Methodology
 
1. Psychrometric analysis of target drying conditions — temperature, humidity, throughput
2. First-principles sizing of compressor, evaporator, condenser, fan, and enclosure insulation
3. Thermal modelling of heat pump cycle and recirculating airflow path
4. Economic payback analysis vs LPG and resistive electric alternatives
5. SolidWorks 3D modelling of the window-unit assembly with modular mounting interface
6. Concept render generated for visual communication of the form factor
 
## Target Applications
 
- **Spice and herb drying** — high value-per-kg crops where quality affects price
- **Fruit and vegetable preservation** — post-harvest loss reduction at farm level
- **Fish and meat drying** — off-grid coastal and rural food preservation
- **Grain drying** — moisture reduction to safe storage levels
- **Solar PV-integrated operation** — compressor load matches small PV panel output
